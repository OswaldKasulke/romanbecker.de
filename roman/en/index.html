A professional valuation lays the foundation for successful marketing. The market-appropriate price results from various factors such as size, year of construction, condition, fittings, location and local demand. Only with a realistic price assessment can we together develop the optimal sales strategy for your property.

The standard land value (Bodenrichtwert) is an average value for undeveloped land in a specific location, determined by the local valuation committee – it is published every two years and serves primarily as a tax reference value. However, it says nothing about what can actually be achieved on the open market.

In practice, due to sharply increased construction costs, buyers and developers calculate with significant discounts: even in sought-after locations such as Cologne-Hahnwald or Cologne-Marienburg, discounts of 20 to 30% on the standard land value have become standard market practice – because the economics of new construction at today's building costs would otherwise no longer work.

For land sellers, this means: the standard land value is not an achievable sale price but a starting point. A realistic assessment requires an individual market valuation – which I am happy to provide free of charge.
